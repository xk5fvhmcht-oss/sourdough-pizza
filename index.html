Spiral mix — don't overdo it
Everything in together: flour, water, ripe starter, then salt. No autolyse, no folds. Spiral runs warm, so aim for a dough temp near 76°F — start with water at about 72°F. Stop at a smooth, just-windowpane dough; sourdough develops fast and a spiral can blow past it.
🔴
Short rise, then ball & chill
4 h on the counter, then divide, ball tight, oil, seal, and into the fridge for 24 h. The cold is where the flavour comes from.
👉
Temper & the finger test — this is your real timer
Pull the balls 1–2 hours before baking (far longer than bread's 20 min — cold dough tears when you open it thin). Ready when a floured fingertip leaves a slow-filling dent. Springs right back → give it more time. Stays put and slack → you're at the edge, bake now.

Calibration internals
Pizza is pulled before the dough fully peaks — that's the “before bread” offset. Lower it for a younger, springier ball; raise it for a more open, fermented one. Your saved “perfect” bakes override this automatically.

How it reasons. Each phase of your schedule delivers a number of starter “doublings” — fast at the counter, roughly 5× slower at 37°F. The dough is ready at a fixed maturity, so more starter needs fewer doublings: that's the whole solve. Your bread (20% starter, pulled at peak) sets the baseline; pizza lands a notch earlier so there's gluten left to stretch. The three lines are your starter's good-day / typical / slow-day moods — the gap between them is the app being honest that it's guessing, and it tightens every time you log a bake. It assumes Caputo 00 and a 100% starter, and past ~48 h the cold turns tangy and the dough gets fragile. Watch the ball, not the clock — that's what the finger test is for.
